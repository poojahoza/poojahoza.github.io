Bio

Pooja Oza is a research assistant at The University of New Hampshire, where she is receiving her Ph.D. in Computer Science under the guidance of Dr. Laura Dietz and is expected to graduate in August 2024.
Her research lies at the intersection of information retrieval, natural language processing, and deep learning. Her research aims to build innovative neuro-symbolic models that learn interactions between structured symbolic knowledge and unstructured textual documents for various predictive information retrieval and natural language processing tasks. Her work involves designing, developing, and training deep learning models that learn neuro-symbolic representations by identifying relevant interactions between symbolic knowledge and textual content.

Projects
Graph Relevance Networks (GRNs)

IJCKG 2023

How can we incorporate relevant information about what makes symbolic knowledge relevant to search systems?

Symbolic knowledge representations does not include the information about why an entity is relevant to a particular query. We propose Graph Relevance Networks (GRNs) that identifies relevant information from unstructured textual documents and incorporates it with the structured knowledge to generate neuro-symoblic representations. GRN improves over graph attention mechanism to identify relevant connections between structured knowledge and textual documents to improve performance of information retrieval systems.

Knowledge-Enhanced Document Representations (KED)

Can we enhance unstructured textual documents representations by integrating symbolic knowledge in the documents to improve information retrieval tasks?

Dense Passage Retrieval systems utilizes the content of textual documents using embedding space to find relevant documents in response to queries. We propose Knowledge-Enhanced Document representations (KED) model that incorporates relevant LLM-generated knowledge with the textual documents representations to generate enriched document representations that helps to improve information retrieval systems. The overarching idea is that the documents relevant to a query contains knowledge relevant to the query and increasing the influence of this relevant knowledge and decreasing the weightage of irrelevant knowledge in the documents helps to generate document representations that focuses on the query-relevant information.

Addressing Exploratory Information Needs with Relevant Entities

Task Focused IR in the era of Generative AI, 2023

We study a retrieval augmented generation (RAG) system where users quite often seek exploratory queries with no prior knowledge. For such exploratory information needs, a crucial step is to guide the users through the information need. Entities are pivotal knowledge-centric landmarks that play a crucial role in human understanding of the underlying concepts within documents. In our work, we perform three steps of the pipeline to guide the users (1) identify the relevant entities and passages using dense retrieval models, (2) explore the relevant entities to generate text that elaborates the relevance of these entities to compose a response from passages and (3) utilize in-context examples to generate relevance labels for entities.
